# Counter-Offer Script (Vietnam/Asian Company)

**Company:** {COMPANY}
**Current Offer:** {OFFER_VND} / ${OFFER_USD}
**Target:** {TARGET_VND} / ${TARGET_USD}

---

## Cultural Notes

- Indirect approach preferred
- Relationship emphasis over transaction
- Patience expected - decisions take time
- Avoid direct confrontation or ultimatums
- "Face" is important - allow counterparty to save face

---

## Script

### Opening (Warmer, Longer)

"I really appreciate the offer and all the time you've spent helping me understand the role. I've enjoyed learning about {COMPANY} and the team's work."

### Transition (Softer)

"I've been thinking about the compensation, and I'd appreciate your input on something."

### Data Anchor (Less Confrontational)

"From what I've seen in the market for similar roles, the typical range seems to be around {MARKET_RANGE}. I wanted to share this with you to get your perspective."

### Counter (Question Form)

"Given my experience with {RELEVANT_SKILLS}, would it be possible to explore {TARGET_VND} (${TARGET_USD})? I'm very interested in joining and want to make sure we find something that works for both sides."

### Close (Relationship-Focused)

"I really value the opportunity to work with {COMPANY}. Please let me know your thoughts, and I'm happy to discuss further."

---

## Key Differences from Standard Script

| Standard | Vietnam/Asian |
|----------|---------------|
| Statements | Questions |
| Direct counter | Exploratory language |
| Data-driven | Relationship-focused |
| Quick close | Open to discussion |

## Japanese Company Specifics

If negotiating with Japanese company (like Giftee, SUN*):
- Expect slower decision process
- May involve multiple stakeholders
- Benefits often stronger than base salary
- Long-term relationship valued over short-term gains
- Patience and respect are essential

## Tips

- Don't rush the process
- Follow up politely after 3-5 business days
- Express gratitude regardless of outcome
- Keep relationship positive for future opportunities
